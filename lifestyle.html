SMART TOOTHBRUSH

The apps for all smart toothbrushes offer three fundamental features. They monitor how often you brush and show a pressure sensor and timer. The app tracks your brushing frequency by keeping track of how frequently you clean your teeth. When you apply excessive pressure to your teeth and gums, the pressure sensor in the app alerts you.

ROBO-BIRDS

The first robotic birds, like the live-flying RoBird, were created by researchers at the University of Twente. New theoretical insights form the basis for the creation of the next generation of robotic birds. His five-year project is ultimately focused on creating a new robotic bird. The earliest example is from 350 BCE, when the mathematician Archytas of Tarentum, who some believe to be the father of mechanics, is credited with building a mechanical wooden dove that could fly up to 200 meters on the power of compressed air or internal steam.

ALBERT CLOCK

An interesting feature of the LED clock known as the Albert Clock is that the hours and minutes are shown as mathematical equations. To prevent repetition, an algorithm was built into the special clock that creates equation terms at random. Alex Schindlbeck, the product designer, claims that Albert Einstein would have likely also become a good mathematician if he had owned a clock like this. While attending an art school in Germany, he made the clock. He took inspiration from Einstein for the clock because he believed the inventor reveled in his work with "childlike delight".

SMART HELMET

Using Arduino and a transmitter, a smart helmet is created (Figure 7). It displays a notification on the LCD and sends an SMS to a registered number with the person's current location if there is alcohol concentration in the breath. Smart helmets are in charge of drowsiness, speed, and blink detection for accident avoidance, and they also send out signals to warn of impending danger. The smart helmets use the rescue request feature to alert authorities to a traffic collision and, in the event of one, to request assistance via text or phone calls.

LIFESAVER DRONE

Surf lifesavers can use Little Ripper, a drone, to find and save swimmers in need by offering aerial observation and liferafts. Kevin Weldon created it after seeing a UAV look for survivors in the wake of Hurricane Katrina. When a shark is spotted nearby, the Shark Spotter drone is set up to fly above open waters and alert surfers and swimmers. The drone is equipped with loudspeakers to accomplish this. A lifeguard will be alerted as soon as the drone detects a shark in the water. The lifeguard can then shout down to those in the water to get to dry land using the loudspeakers.
